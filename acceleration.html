Acceleration Details

Definition:

Acceleration is the rate at which the velocity of an object changes over time.

Formula:

The formula for acceleration (a) is given by:

a = (vf - vi) / t

Where:

  • a: Acceleration
  • vf: Final velocity
  • vi: Initial velocity
  • t: Time

Units:

The SI unit of acceleration is meters per second squared (m/s2).

Types of Acceleration:

  • Positive Acceleration: Speed of an object is increasing.
  • Negative (Deceleration): Speed of an object is decreasing.
  • Uniform Acceleration: Constant rate of change of velocity.

Examples:

Examples of acceleration include a car speeding up, a ball falling under gravity, or a rocket taking off.

Understanding acceleration is crucial in analyzing the motion of objects and is a fundamental concept in physics.
