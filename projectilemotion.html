Projectile Motion

Components of Motion:

  • Horizontal Motion: The horizontal motion is uniform and constant.
  • Vertical Motion: The vertical motion is influenced by gravity.

Trajectory:

The path that a projectile follows is called its trajectory. The trajectory is typically a curved path.

Initial Velocity and Launch Angle:

  • Initial Velocity: The speed at which the projectile is launched.
  • Launch Angle: The angle at which the projectile is launched with respect to the horizontal.

Range:

The range of a projectile is the horizontal distance it travels before hitting the ground.

Maximum Height:

The maximum height reached by the projectile is determined by the vertical component of the initial velocity and the acceleration due to gravity.

Equations of Motion:

Equations for horizontal and vertical motion are used to describe the trajectory.

Symmetry:

The time of flight for the projectile is determined by the vertical motion alone.

Air Resistance:

In basic projectile motion analysis, air resistance is often ignored.

Projectile motion is a fundamental concept in physics and is used to understand the motion of objects like thrown balls, launched projectiles, and even celestial bodies like planets in certain scenarios.
